CSII India’s Right to Information (RTI) System is a digital governance solution designed to simplify and streamline the RTI request process for public authorities and citizens. The system ensures timely response to information requests, promotes administrative accountability, and supports proactive disclosure in compliance with the RTI Act, 2005.

Key Features & Functionalities

Right to Access Information Empowers citizens to request information from any public authority.
Covers All Public Authorities Includes government departments, ministries, PSUs, and NGOs receiving public funds.
Defined Time Limit Information must be provided within 30 days (or 48 hours for life and liberty-related matters).
Appointment of Public Information Officers (PIOs) Every public authority must appoint PIOs to process RTI requests.
Low Application Fee Minimal fee (varies by state); free for BPL (Below Poverty Line) applicants.
Exemptions and Exceptions Certain sensitive information (like national security, personal privacy) is exempt under Section 8 of the Act.
Appeals and Complaints Process If unsatisfied, applicants can appeal to First Appellate Authority and then the Central/State Information Commission.
Promotes Transparency and Accountability Helps reduce corruption and ensures better governance by making the system more open.

Understanding Right to Information (RTI) System

The Right to Information Act empowers Indian citizens to request information from public authorities to ensure openness in governance. However, handling these requests manually can be time-consuming, error-prone, and non-compliant.

CSII India's RTI System automates the entire lifecycle—from application to appeal—creating a centralized, auditable, and citizen-friendly mechanism that enhances government responsiveness and public trust.
